## 1 The computer is disappearing. It's gone from the early days of multi-tonne adding machine to being small enough to embed in a pair of wireless earphones. Along the way they've become so pervasive as to be invisible. We stop recognising them because they are ubiquitous.

## 2 The presence of a computer in every device has contributed to the notion that software is eating the world: more and more tasks that were undertaken in the real world with atoms are achieved by switching digital bits. They have become easier to use and more powerful, but how far can we take it?

Steve Jobs famously visited their lab one day, and some months later the Apple Lisa, the Mac's ancestor, was born.
